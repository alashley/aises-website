About Us

The MIT Chapter of the American Indian Science and Engineering Society [AISES] is a student-run group aimed at promoting Native culture and community here on campus, while also promoting STEM related education to various tribal communities. We accomplish this through:

  • Creating a social framework that allows students to benefit from one another
  • Strengthening native culture by sharing our personal knowledge and experiences
Some of our highlighted events include:
  • Trips to various National Conferences
  • Interaction with tribes in the New England area
  • Native student exposure in the STEM field
For the students on campus, we also hold plenty of community events:

  • Weekly Lounge Hours, where we have tribal news periodicals and snacks.
  • Monthly Dinner Meetings
  • Movie Nights (Ever watched Star Wars in Navajo?)
Group membership is open to any MIT-affiliate, and many of our events are open to the public! Please contact us at aises-request@mit.edu for any questions or concerns!



Our Mission Statement

Our mission is to establish a supportive community for native students at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, as well as network with other AISES chapters.
